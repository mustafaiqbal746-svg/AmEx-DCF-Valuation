## 🔍 Methodology

**Three-Statement Model**
- Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement fully linked across 5 historical years and 5 projected years
- Balance sheet balances to zero every year (cash as plug for projection years)
- Cash flow ties exactly to the change in cash on the balance sheet
- Revenue projected from 9.0% growth tapering to 7.0% terminal

**DCF Valuation**
- Unlevered free cash flow = NOPAT + D&A − CapEx − ΔNWC
- WACC built from CAPM cost of equity (10.25%) and after-tax cost of debt (4.07%), weighted by capital structure
- Terminal value via Gordon Growth method
- Discount factors applied to mid-year convention

**Sensitivity Analysis**
- 5×5 matrix flexing WACC (8.0%–10.0%) against terminal growth (2.0%–4.0%)
- Implied price range: $243 – $418

**Comparable Company Analysis**
- 6 consumer finance peers: Visa (V), Mastercard (MA), Capital One (COF), Discover (DFS), Synchrony (SYF), Ally (ALLY)
- EV/Revenue, P/E (NTM), and P/B multiples
- Implied valuation cross-check against DCF

## 💡 Investment Thesis

American Express operates a structurally advantaged closed-loop payments network combined with a premium card lending franchise. The thesis rests on three pillars:

1. **Premium cardholder economics** — Platinum, Gold, and Centurion cardholders generate 3-4× the spend of mass-market cards, supporting durable discount revenue (~55% of total revenue)
2. **Closed-loop network** — AXP captures economics on both card issuance and merchant acquiring, unlike Visa/Mastercard which earn only network fees
3. **Self-funded growth** — A growing deposit base (~$137B in FY2024) provides low-cost funding for the Card Member Loans portfolio

At a 17.6× P/E on FY25E EPS, the stock is fairly valued relative to its growth profile — supporting a HOLD rating with constructive bias on a pullback to $260.

## ⚠️ Key Risks

- **Consumer credit cycle** — Recession would drive provision expense materially higher; FY24 provision was 8.2% of revenue and could exceed 12% in stress
- **Premium spend slowdown** — Macro pressure on affluent consumers would compress discount revenue
- **Competitive intensity** — Chase Sapphire Reserve and Capital One Venture X have closed the premium card gap
- **Regulatory** — CFPB scrutiny of late fees and interchange
